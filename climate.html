Climate Data Analysis — Cleaning, Modeling, and Visualization

Processed large climate datasets end-to-end: data cleaning and transformation, exploratory analysis, curve fitting, and visualization to evaluate relationships between geographic and climate indicators.

Project context

Completed for Computational Modeling and Data Analysis I. The goal was to take real-world climate data, make it usable (cleaning + transformations), and build models to understand relationships among variables such as elevation, precipitation, and other climate indicators, while clearly communicating uncertainty and limitations.

What I did

• Cleaned and transformed raw climate datasets using pandas (missing values, inconsistent formats, filtering, and feature construction).
• Performed exploratory analysis to identify trends, outliers, and potential confounders before fitting models.
• Applied curve fitting / regression-style modeling to quantify relationships between key indicators.
• Produced visualizations (scatter, trend lines, residual-style checks) to support interpretation and assess robustness.
• Documented model assumptions and data limitations (sampling issues, noise, and correlation vs causation).

Key takeaways

This project emphasized that modeling quality starts with data quality. Small preprocessing decisions (filters, transformations, and handling missing values) strongly affect conclusions.
